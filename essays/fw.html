On On Freelancing Well

Your priority is not to get more clients, it’s to get better clients

Your biggest assets are your portfolio, reputation and network. Build them.

Get comfortable riding the wave through busy and quiet times.

One freelancer asks, “What do you want me to do?” Another asks, “What are you trying to achieve?” One is stuck in a race to the bottom. Another is charging on value.

Don’t try to do work like everyone else. Develop a style, one that hopefully divides opinion.

Social media is not essential. The best gigs come from people you know personally.

Word of mouth is the game. Being great to work with fuels it.

Do creative projects for yourself. They keep your soul alive, and their authenticity ironically makes them your best marketing tools.

You can get what you want if you help others get what they want.

Never stop broadening your skillset and experimenting. Be ready to change direction at any moment.

Learn how to sell, price projects and write proposals. This can’t be learned in a classroom. Go out and do it. Each mistake is a lesson.

Don’t outsource the creative work.

Build a process for every part of your business.

Keep track of every single contact, conversation and enquiry. Keep in touch regularly.

Figure out how to become more valuable to your best clients.
