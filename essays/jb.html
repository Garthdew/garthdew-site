On Just Being

I built a den with my kids yesterday.

When it was done, I sat inside with my eldest.

She said, "What would we do all day if we lived here?"

It got me thinking.

For most of humankind's existence, the only focus was not dying.

But now, we've built a world that makes that much easier.

So, we distract ourselves with stories about politics, work, status, money, purpose and so on.

All of it just concepts.

And so when my daughter asked me that question...

I reflected on the superfluousness of the things we take so seriously.

Now, isn't this a great realisation to live from?

Like, if you could strip away all the stories and conditioning...

And create life from a blank canvas...

As your true self...

What type of life would you live?

What would be possible for you?

My response to my daughter:

"We'd just BE."
