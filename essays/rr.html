On Robert Rodriguez

When filmmaker Robert Rodriguez was 23, he subjected himself to medical trials to fund his first feature length film.

His idea was to make a practice film for less than $7000 before going on to make his first 'proper' film.

He wrote the script whilst spending a month locked up in a medical facility.

He structured it around the things he had at his disposal.

From friends who could act, to a Mexican ranch, a bus, a turtle and a bar.

After leaving the medical facility, he borrowed a camera and bought $7000 of film stock.

Then he got to it.

He did everything himself - from producing to directing, shooting and editing.

He pulled in favours, broke the rules and did what no one else thought was possible.

It was a total guerrilla filmmaking.

The result was his first film, "El Mariachi."

What happened next was incredible.

He cut a trailer for the film, hoping to sell it to the Spanish video market, and started showing it to studios.

It was so good, Columbia Pictures offered to buy and release the film.

And they signed Rodriguez to write and direct films for them.

El Mariachi was then entered into Sundance, and won.

This catapulted Rodriguez into the big time.

He has gone on to be one of the most innovative and successful filmmakers of all time.

All whilst sharing his process and inspiring others to pursue a creative life.

Here are some of the bits of advice I try to live by:

1. Take stock of what you already have at your disposal, and use that to drive your project forward.

2. Forget about rules and conventions. They are there to be broken.

3. Be a generalist. By learning to do everything, you can save money and have more creative control.

4. Take on lots of meaningful projects. By chipping away at each one throughout the week, you make continual progress and can live your dream.

5. Live a creative life. You can put your creativity into everything - from business, to art and parenting.

If you want to learn more about this story, Rodriguez wrote a book about it called "Rebel Without A Crew."
