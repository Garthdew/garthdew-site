On Fun And Play

When I got home, I wasn't in the mood for being Dad.

Sometimes, you just want to chill.

But then I remembered a question I heard that day:

"How can you lean more in to fun and play?"

I love that queston.

Because when I'm having fun, everything is more creative, effortless and amazing.

So I snapped out of it.

And I declared 6PM, "The most fun hour of the day."

I danced like a lunatic with the kids, played twister and acted out an episode of Bluey with some figurines.

At bath time, my daughter taught me an Abba song they'd learned in school.

"Su-per per. Troo-per per!"

We sang it together.

A couple of hours flew by. We had a good laugh. Everyone went to bed happy.

It beat forcing them to watch TV and being a grumpy sod.

So, I set a daily alarm for 6PM.

Which is now officially "The most fun hour of the day."

There's a lot of power in living from a context like that.

And this doesn't only apply to home life.

How would you show up if you made your work more fun?

  • What content would you put out?
  • How would you describe your service?
  • What would you do to make your clients smile?

I wonder:

Would coming from that place make more impact on your bottom line?

Compared to a bland outreach strategy or generic content template?

So...

How will you lean in to fun and play today?
