On The Barbell Strategy

Have you heard of Nassim Taleb's barbell strategy?

It's one of my favourite takeaways from his book Antifragile.

It's about being wary of the “middle path” in business and life.

What often looks like balance is actually risk, disguised as safety.

Take investing.

We are told to stick our funds in a tracker.

But what happens when a Black Swan (big/unpredictable) event hits?

A tweet, a tariff, a pandemic, and 20% of your pension can disappear.

It's the same in business.

I see a lot of creatives trying to build studios/agencies.

Hiring staff. Growing overheads.

It looks like a smart next step.

But most just end up with more stress, less profit, and no freedom.

This is where the barbell comes in.

On one side (majority): caution and safety.

  • One person
  • Sell a service
  • Ability to pivot
  • Steady cash flow
  • A dozen great clients
  • High interest cash accounts
  • Simple operation, low overheads

On the other (minority): risky bets with big potential upside.

  • Creating experimental art
  • Side projects that can scale
  • Cryptocurrency and speculative stocks
  • Wild experiments that could change everything

And nothing in the middle.

That way, you remove the risk of complete ruin, but open yourself up to big potential returns.

Another good example is running.

A proven strategy is to run at a very low intensity for 80% of your training.

Then train very hard for 20%.

Meaning you build your aerobic base slowly and safely, while pushing your limits just enough to improve speed and power.

No grinding at a “fast but not fast enough” pace every day.

No burnout. No injuries. No false sense of progress.

Avoid the middle.

That’s how the real gains are made.

This isn't true, but it could be useful.
