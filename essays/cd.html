On The Creative's Delimma

There is a conflict inside commercial creatives.

They were once artists.

They created just to create.

Then, one day, they chose to make a living from their craft.

They started trading creative labour for money.

When this happens, the work cannot stay pure.

This is not wrong.

We all need money, so why not earn it doing what you enjoy?

But over time, commercial work is all you do.

You stop creating just to create.

Your craft becomes a job, like washing the dishes or cutting the grass.

You start to loathe the thing you once loved.

This causes a conflict inside you.

As time goes on, you get so into making money from your craft that the mind blocks any attempt at art.

“What’s the point?”

"Why do this when I could be working?”

“My art is bad. Let’s stick to client work.”

The longer you walk this path, the more you hurt your soul.

And the less creative you become.

This is why I make time for my own art.

Some music here, a street photo there.

It keeps the fire alive.

It is the best thing you can do for yourself and your clients.

So you can bring your magic to everything you do.

Your art is not trivial. Do not treat it that way.
