On The Gift Of Parenting

As parents, we are told to do our duty.

To be there for our children.

To provide for our children.

To love, cherish and entertain our children.

It's our duty.

But who wants to do duties?

No one.

When we're doing our duties, we'd rather be doing something else.

Duties are hard.

They require effort.

And when there is effort, or forced behavior, there is internal conflict.

Because there is duality.

And when there is conflict, there can't be awareness, peace or happiness.

So... when you treat parenting as a duty, it becomes a chore.

You're constantly looking ahead to when you will be alone.

And you miss the magic.

You see, kids are not a duty.

They are an opportunity.

An opportunity to unlearn your conditioning.

An opportunity to rediscover your imagination.

An opportunity to live completely in the moment.

To have no concept of the past or future.

To be totally engaged.

To have no concept of what should be.

Only to know what is.

Children can teach us more than we can teach them.

Not because they have something we don't.

But because they don't have something we have.

The mind and all its programming, anxiety, hate, fear, greed and stress.

What if you treated parenting as an opportunity to live completely in the moment?

All you have to do is go onto their level, and enter their world.

You will be fully engaged.

When you are fully engaged, you lose the mind.

Then you can connect with your child in peace, and develop a true relationship with no ideas of what should be.

Only experiencing what is, and the joy of this very moment.
