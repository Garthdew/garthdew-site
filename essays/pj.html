On Living With Peace And Joy

It was the middle of the pandemic.

Midnight.

I was on the couch with a large bourbon.

My mind was in pieces.

I was looking after my toddler full-time.

Unsure if my business would survive.

Worried about family.

Stressed.

That night, I stumbled on a lecture by Anthony De Mello.

He was answering the question:

How do you live with peace and joy?

It hit me hard.

I took notes and found them again recently.

To live with peace and joy:

  1. Be me.
  2. Be now.
  3. Be here.

Be me

Drop prejudices, preconceptions, and programming.

Observe myself.

Ask, "Who is that person?"

Ask, "What voices are they listening to?"

Question what you spend your time on.

Are you a robot?

Or are you living?

Be now

How much time do you spend in the past or the future?

The past isn’t real.

The future isn’t real.

It’s all in your head.

Come into the now.

Don't clean the cup to drink the coffee.

Clean the cup.

Focus on breathing in and breathing out.

Be present.

Be engaged.

Do things to experience life.

Walking.

Playing.

Reading.

Cooking.

Creating.

Conversing.

Observe.

Take it all in.

Be here

Come out of the head and into the senses.

Come out of distraction and into experience.

Ideas and abstractions are not life.

Experience is life.

Tasting.

Seeing.

Hearing.

Look at the wonder of a child seeing something new.

"The day we teach the child the name of the bird is the day the child stops seeing the bird." (Krishnamurti.)

The bird becomes an idea in their mind.

They miss its uniqueness.

The name is the obstacle to seeing.

The answer:

See the bird right now.

Listen to the sound of the bird right now.

Enlightenment

When I see, I see.

When I hear, I hear.

When I touch, I touch.

So...

Observe yourself.

Stop the puppet act.

Experience reality.

Come to your senses.
