On Our Internal Stories

The stories we tell ourselves shape our reality.

I caught myself in a familiar loop the other day.

That voice saying "You're so unfocussed. Pick a lane."

But then I remembered—these stories aren't facts. They're just default programming running in the background.

In conversation, I often hear so many limiting narratives:

"I'm too old"

"I'll be happy when..."

"I'm not experienced enough"

"I don't have the confidence to do that"

All stories. Not facts.

What if we rewrote these scripts? What if we lived from a place of possibility instead?

"I have such a wealth of experience to draw from"

"I choose to be happy today no matter what happens"

"Age is not a factor, I am good enough to deliver for this client"

"I am complete and whole, and I fully believe in myself and my abilities"

I tried this approach with my own story.

Rather than "scattered," I started seeing myself as "curious."

Instead of "unfocused," I became a "doer of projects."

The shift was subtle at first.

But over time, the new story changed how I showed up.

I stopped apologising for exploring multiple interests.

And started embracing the connections between them.

That's the thing about stories—they create the lens through which we see everything.

Change your stories, change your life.

What story are you telling yourself today?
