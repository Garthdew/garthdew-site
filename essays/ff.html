On Flow Over Fear

The most fun I had playing rugby was when I went to high school at 11.

It was pure flow and joy with my friends. 

At 13, representative rugby and competitions were introduced.

Over the next 7 years, I won the cups and got the caps.

From Lancashire U13s to England U19s.

But the more I achieved externally, the more the fun faded internally.

And I started to play from a place of fear rather than flow.

I wouldn't change any of those experiences.

I was so fortunate to have them.

But what I've learned is that operating from a place of fear is so limiting to our potential.

If we could let go of our fears in our personal and professional lives...

How much more authentic, expansive, joyful and rich could they be?
