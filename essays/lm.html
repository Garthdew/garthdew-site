On Treating Life Like Music

When I was 8, my dad played me the Otis Blue album.

I loved it.

When I was 11, I heard Americana by The Offspring.

Otis was no longer cool.

In high school, my hormones changed.

I got moodier.

Too moody even for The Offspring.

So I listened to Rage Against the Machine and Slipknot.

At 15, I started going to parties.

Dance music finally made sense.

Now, at 37, I explore new sounds all the time.

Nothing is off limits.

My point?

With music, we’re fine dropping an artist or genre when it no longer fits.

We don’t keep playing the same songs just because we always have.

That would seem limiting. And stupid.

But we do it in life...

Especially when we think the stakes are higher.

We stick with projects we no longer care about.

We stay in relationships that hold us back.

We believe things that no longer help us grow.

All because we've done it for so long.

But what if we treated life like music?

What if we engaged fully in something...

Then let go when it ran its course?

To make room for the next wonderful thing.

What impact would that have?

Treat life like music.
