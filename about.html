About

I've never liked the idea of "staying in my lane." Labels and titles are limiting. I'm more verb than noun. If pressed for a title, I'd choose "doer." 

Playing pro rugby. Making films. Recording music. Taking photographs. Writing. Running. Building businesses. Creating experiences. Guiding others. Following my curiosity. 

For over a decade, I've run a video studio telling stories for global brands. Recently, I've shifted toward creating original art. I split my time between client projects and personal work—following what makes me feel alive. Sometimes it leads to a film, sometimes a business, sometimes a run through the hills. 

All my projects stem from one question: What happens when I stop performing and start being myself?

Timeline

0-11: Being a kid.

11-18: Obsessed with rugby. Set and achieved a goal of playing international schoolboy rugby.

18-23: Joined Sale Sharks. Played in the Junior Rugby World Cup. After contract release, pivoted to music production while playing semi-pro. Post-graduation, a devastating knee injury forced retirement.

23-26: Hit rock bottom. Started in a sportswear warehouse, eventually moving to their office as a video producer. Learned filmmaking on the job and rebuilt my life.

26-36: Founded my own video studio. Became head coach at Preston Grasshoppers. Met my wife. Bought a house. Welcomed our first child. Left rugby to focus on business. Built a house. Discovered running. Had our second child. Grew my business. Explored spirituality and prioritised happiness. A transformative decade.

Now: Running my video studio and embracing fatherhood. Exploring new projects and ways of being. Feel authentically myself for the first time since childhood.
