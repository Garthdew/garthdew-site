About

I've never liked the idea of "staying in my lane."

Labels and titles are limiting.

I'm more verb than noun. If pressed for a title, I'd choose doer of projects.

Some projects span years: becoming a professional rugby player, building a video studio that tells stories for global brands.

Others are brief but intense: running an ultramarathon, recording an ambient album, capturing street photography, creating an event.

I spend my days making things — sometimes for clients, sometimes for myself.

Films, music, photos, tools, experiences.

Every project begins with curiosity and brings me closer to who I really am.

My life unfolds inside the question: What happens when I stop performing and just be myself?

Timeline

0-11: Being a kid.

11-18: Obsessed with rugby. Set and achieved a goal of playing international schoolboy rugby.

18-23: Joined Sale Sharks. Played in the Junior Rugby World Cup. After contract release, pivoted to a music production degree while playing semi-pro. Post-graduation, a devastating knee injury forced retirement.

23-26: Hit rock bottom. Started working in a sportswear company warehouse, eventually moving to their office as a video producer. Learned filmmaking on the job and rebuilt my life.

26-36: Founded my own video studio. Became head coach at Preston Grasshoppers. Met my wife. Bought a house. Welcomed our first child. Left rugby to focus on business. Built a house. Discovered running. Had our second child. Grew my business. Explored spirituality and prioritised happiness. A transformative decade.

Now: Running my video studio and embracing fatherhood. Exploring new projects and ways of being. Feel authentically myself for the first time since childhood.
