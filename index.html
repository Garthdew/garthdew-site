I’ve never liked the idea of picking a lane.
I’m a filmmaker but I’m not in “film.”
A musician, but not in the “music industry.”
A photographer, but not a professional one.
A coach, but not here to fix anyone.
A runner, but I don’t track my miles.
An artist, maybe — mostly I just follow curiosity.

A doer of things.

I’ve run a video studio for over a decade. Made films for brands you’d know and ones you wouldn’t.
But the work I care about most comes when I’m not trying to prove anything — just noticing, creating, playing.

That’s how my best work happens:
Not by performing, but by being.

These days I split my time between client projects and my own.
I create experiences for others — coaching, gatherings, creative spaces.
I read, write, photograph, compose, move.
I follow what feels alive. Sometimes it leads to a film, sometimes a zine, sometimes a run through the hills.

Everything I make is part of the same experiment:
What happens when you stop performing, and start being yourself?
