Festival

Photographs made at a British festival, 2016

I don’t remember why I brought a cheap compact camera to the festival that year. Probably to avoid damaging anything expensive. It was before I had children and a mortgage. A limbo time when escapism felt essential.

These photographs sat forgotten on an SD card in an old bag for nearly a decade. But when I found them again, they struck me as something more than casual snapshots. There was an authenticity and rawness I couldn’t ignore.

The images are both amusing and uncomfortable to look back on. They capture a version of myself I miss — but not one I want to return to.
